VARAHA GHAT

VARAHA GHAT: is situated in the south-western corner of Sri Vrindavan on the old bank of Yamuna. At Varaha Ghata, Krsna manifested His form as the transcedental boar Varaha for the pleasure of the gopis. In Satya Yuga, the demon Hiranyaksa had disturbed the balance of the earth, ravaging its resources by mining; thus, it fell out of orbit to the buttom of universe. Since the earth had fallen into an impure condition, the Lord assumed the form of a boar and picked up Mother Earth on His tusks. He reinstated the earthly planet and killed the demon Hiranyaksa. VARAHA TEMPLE: is situated at VARAHA GHAT and is recently renovated, it is now a compound with a red sandstone temple, asrama, and courtyard. Within the temple are beautiful Deities of Lord Varaha, Sita Ram Laksmana, Hanuman, and Radha Krsna. The deities are now worshipped by devotees of Ramanuja Samprodaya.
